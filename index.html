01The Person Behind the Work

How I Think

I've never believed data and creativity sit on opposite sides of the room.

The numbers tell me what's actually happening, but a number has never, on its own, made anyone care enough to act. That's the job of the narrative. So I hold both at once: I'll run the model, then say the one clear thing that turns analysis into a decision someone is willing to stand behind. Insight that stays trapped in a slide isn't strategy to me; it's just an opinion with charts.

I view brands not as static logos, but as living conversations, built through data, shaped by storytelling, and scaled through strategy.

I work across every stage of a brand's life. Hand me a category with no U.S. market intelligence and I'll get into the field, talk to real people, and build the strategy from scratch; hand me an established, data-rich business and I'll live inside its analytics, surfacing the next point of growth, sharpening the positioning, and keeping the marketing engine measured and accountable, month after month. Building from the ground up or compounding what already works energizes me equally; only the starting line changes.

I'm rigorous about the truth: I'll fact-check a figure to the dollar before it goes near a stakeholder, and I'm impatient with work that never ships. I move across cultures and categories the same way I move across spreadsheets and stories: looking for the pattern, then the point.

My ideology
Hungry, humble, smart, and always a proactive learner.
I believe
Data earns trust. Story earns action. I refuse to trade one for the other.
I believe
Ambiguity isn't a wall. It's an un-framed question.
I believe
A strategy that never ships is just a nicely formatted opinion.
